About

Originally from Scotland, Blair is an accomplished musician based in Nashville, TN, via Berlin, and Brooklyn.

As a drummer, Blair has toured and recorded with a broad range of acclaimed artists, such as Findlay Napier, Rachel Sermanni, Daniel Thorpe, Katie Sutherland, Hamish Napier, Hannah Read, The Ansion, Miss Tess And The Talkbacks, Nora Jane Struthers, Z&A, Rachel Baiman, Brooms and Emma Beaton and Nic Gareiss, to name a few.

He has also worked with acclaimed producers, including Steve Chrisanthou (Corinne Bailey Rae), David Kosten (Coldplay, The Flaming Lips, Bat For Lashes), Matty Benbrook (Paulo Nutini, Dido) and Alex James of Blur.

Live, Blair has appeared at a number of major festivals in the U.K and U.S.A including, T in the Park, Rockness, Celtic Connections, Secret Garden Party, Glasgow Jazz Festival, Edinburgh Fringe Festival, Floyd Fest, Telluride Bluegrass Festival, Fibark Festival and New York Tartan Week. He has also shared the stage with artists such as, Sir Elton John, Blondie, Jim Lauderdale, Idlewild, Candi Staton, Teenage Fanclub and many more, as well as TV and radio appearances worldwide from the BBC TV and radio to Music City Roots for PBS in the United States. Blair's drums have also featured in a number of TV advertising campaigns across the world, most notably this 2010 commercial for Victoria's Secret in New York.

-------------------

Aside from working with original artists, Blair worked as the principle percussionist onboard the Disney Dream cruise ship for itâ€™s inaugural sailings in 2011. From there, he went onto work with acclaimed theatre show, The 12 Tenors, which toured Europe and China extensively between 2012 and 2013.

Off stage, Blair has a wealth of teaching experience and has taught at summer schools across the U.K as well as teaching a Yamaha Music School U.K and Queen Victoria Military school in Dunblane, Scotland.

Blair currently endorses Duallist drum pedals, Protection Racket cases and the Cajon Brush by Paul Jennings.

-------------------

In 2014, Blair won the Nairn Book Festival Songwriting Competition judged by accomplished Scottish songwriters Gillian Frame and Findlay Napier. Since arriving in the United States, Blair has appeared as a solo artist on numerous occasions in New York and Nashville as well as appearing at the 2014 Campfire Festival at Club Passim in Cambridge, MA.

In the summer of 2015, Blair toured the west coast of the United States and Canada with David Moss and Emma Beaton in support of his debut release, Religious Song For Non Believers, a collection of original material which he self-released in June 2015.

-------------------

Aside from his musical persuits, Blair played a supporting role in the 2015 short film, Bumdom, written and directed by award winning director Matt Cascella.
